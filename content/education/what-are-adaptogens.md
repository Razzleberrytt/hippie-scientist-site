"Adaptogen" is a framing term for plants studied for their potential to help the
body cope with stress and return to balance (homeostasis). The concept centers on
the **HPA axis** — the hormonal loop that governs cortisol and the stress
response.

The strongest human evidence sits with a few well-studied botanicals such as
ashwagandha and rhodiola, where randomized trials report modest reductions in
perceived stress. For most other "adaptogens," evidence is preliminary,
mechanism-led, or extrapolated from animal models.

Treat the label as a starting point for investigation, not a guarantee. Adaptogen
effects are usually subtle, build over weeks, and depend heavily on sleep, load,
and individual variability.
