Short bursts of stress sharpen attention and memory. The problem is **chronic**
activation. Sustained cortisol exposure biases the brain toward the amygdala
(threat detection) and away from the prefrontal cortex (planning and
self-regulation), which is why prolonged stress feels like brain fog, reactivity,
and poor sleep.

Over time this "allostatic load" can blunt recovery systems. The useful reframe is
that the target is not zero stress but **better recovery** — sleep continuity,
downshifting the nervous system, and reducing total load.

Supplements discussed in this context are best understood as small supports inside
that larger recovery picture, not replacements for it.
