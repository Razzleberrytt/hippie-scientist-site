# Optional structured study context that a page can spread into
# <StudyDesignSnapshot {...frontmatter.pivotalStudy} />. Kept out of the
# component so the same component can be reused anywhere.
pivotalStudy:
  grade: Moderate
  summary: A few small human trials point the same way, but the evidence is thinner — promising, not settled.
  gradeRationale: A few randomized trials show a consistent signal, but small samples and short durations limit confidence.
  studyType: Several small randomized, placebo-controlled trials
  population: Modest adult samples
  participants: Dozens per trial
  duration: 4–8 weeks
  comparator: Placebo
  limitations:
    - Small samples widen the uncertainty around the true effect.
    - Short trials cannot speak to long-term use.
---

A grade on this site is shorthand for **how good the human evidence is** — not a
promise about how well something will work for you. The `<StudyDesignSnapshot>`
component keeps the practical takeaway prominent and moves the reasoning — the
grade rationale, trial design, and limitations — into an optional, expandable
panel.

## Minimal embed

The only required prop is `summary`. Add a `grade` and you have a useful block:

<StudyDesignSnapshot
  grade="Moderate"
  summary="A few small human trials suggest a real but modest effect."
/>

## Fuller embed

Pass the convenience design props (`studyType`, `participants`, `duration`, …)
plus `limitations` to explain *why* a grade was assigned:

<StudyDesignSnapshot
  grade="Moderate"
  gradeRationale="Several randomized trials show a consistent signal, but the trials are small and short."
  summary="Standardized extract appears to help sleep quality, with modest, replicated effects."
  studyType="Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled"
  population="Adults with poor sleep"
  participants="~60 per trial"
  duration="6–8 weeks"
  comparator="Placebo"
  dosing="~300–600 mg/day standardized extract"
  limitations={[
    "Small samples and short durations limit confidence in the effect size.",
    "Several trials are manufacturer-funded.",
  ]}
  context="Treat it as a modest, optional support inside a broader routine."
/>

## Driving it from frontmatter

Because the component holds no data of its own, a page can keep the facts in
frontmatter and spread them in — so the same component renders consistently
across profiles, goals, and education content:

```mdx
<StudyDesignSnapshot {...frontmatter.pivotalStudy} />
```

That is the recommended pattern when the study context belongs to the page
rather than to the prose.
