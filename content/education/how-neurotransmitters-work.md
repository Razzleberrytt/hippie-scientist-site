Neurotransmitters are chemical messengers that neurons use to pass signals across
a tiny gap called the synapse. When an electrical impulse reaches the end of a
neuron, it releases neurotransmitter molecules that drift across the synapse and
bind to receptors on the next cell.

The popular idea of a single "feel-good chemical" is misleading. What matters is
the **balance** between excitatory signaling (glutamate), inhibitory signaling
(GABA), and modulatory systems (dopamine, serotonin, acetylcholine). Receptor
sensitivity, reuptake speed, and enzyme activity shape the response as much as
the raw amount of any neurotransmitter.

This systems view is the right lens for thinking about herbs and compounds: most
do not "boost" one chemical so much as nudge a pathway, and individual response
varies widely.
