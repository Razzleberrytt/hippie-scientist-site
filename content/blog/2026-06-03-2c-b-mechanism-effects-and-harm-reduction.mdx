# 2C-B: Mechanism, Effects, and Harm Reduction

2C-B (4-bromo-2,5-dimethoxyphenethylamine) is a synthetic psychedelic phenethylamine first synthesized by Alexander Shulgin in 1974. 

## Highlights
- **Mechanism of Action**: Primary agonist at the 5-HT2C receptor, with moderate affinity for 5-HT2A. Unlike LSD or psilocybin, its unique profile produces a blend of sensory enhancement and mild stimulant effects.
- **Onset and Duration**: Typical onset is 45 to 75 minutes, with active effects lasting between 4 to 8 hours.
- **Harm Reduction Pointers**: Essential set and setting, accurate milligram scaling (due to a steep dose-response curve), and avoiding combination with stimulants or MAOIs.
