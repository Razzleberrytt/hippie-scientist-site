# 7-Hydroxymitragynine vs MGM-15 vs Mitragynine Pseudoindoxyl

> **Strong disclaimer:** This comparison is for evidence transparency and harm reduction only. It is not medical advice, legal advice, dosing guidance, sourcing guidance, or an endorsement of use. All three compounds should be treated as opioid-acting substances with meaningful risk.

## Evidence Snapshot

[7-Hydroxymitragynine](/compounds/7-hydroxymitragynine), [MGM-15](/compounds/dihydro-7-hydroxy-mitragynine-mgm-15), and [mitragynine pseudoindoxyl](/compounds/mitragynine-pseudoindoxyl) are all much more potent opioid-receptor ligands than mitragynine in relevant laboratory contexts. Human safety data are extremely limited for 7-OH and mitragynine pseudoindoxyl, and essentially absent for MGM-15.

The practical harm-reduction conclusion is simple: do not treat any of these as ordinary kratom leaf.

## Key Takeaways

- All three carry real opioid risks.
- None have adequate human safety data for casual or self-directed use.
- Many products are misleadingly marketed as "kratom" despite being concentrated derivatives.
- Product labels may not accurately describe dose, identity, purity, or co-ingredients.
- Mitragynine pseudoindoxyl has documented severe withdrawal concern in case literature.
- MGM-15 has no controlled human clinical safety data.
- 7-OH has more public visibility, but isolated or concentrated 7-OH remains very different from traditional kratom leaf.

## Harm Reduction

> **Avoid products containing 7-OH, MGM-15, or mitragynine pseudoindoxyl.** Treat them as high-potency opioid-acting products, not as dietary supplements.

If exposure has already happened:

- Do not combine with depressants such as alcohol, benzodiazepines, gabapentinoids, sedating antihistamines, opioids, sleep medications, or muscle relaxants.
- Do not drive or use alone.
- Seek emergency care for slowed breathing, blue lips, loss of consciousness, severe sedation, chest pain, collapse, or inability to stay awake.
- Naloxone access is prudent around opioid-acting products, but emergency care remains necessary for overdose symptoms.

## Which Is Riskiest?

There is no responsible way to rank personal safety by anecdote or marketing claims. The evidence-first ranking is by uncertainty and opioid concern:

- **7-OH:** better known, still high concern when concentrated or isolated.
- **MGM-15:** less human data and higher potency concern than 7-OH in preclinical contexts.
- **Mitragynine pseudoindoxyl:** very high receptor-activity concern, limited human data, and severe withdrawal case reports.

For practical decision-making, the distinction does not change the core recommendation: avoid all three in unregulated products.
