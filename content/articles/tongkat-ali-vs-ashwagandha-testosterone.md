## TL;DR

**Tongkat ali and ashwagandha both support testosterone, but through completely different mechanisms.** Tongkat ali may directly stimulate testosterone production — studies show +20-30% in some trials. Ashwagandha increases testosterone indirectly by lowering cortisol — high cortisol suppresses testosterone production. Choose based on your primary issue: low energy/libido with normal stress → tongkat ali; high stress/poor sleep + low testosterone → ashwagandha. Evidence for both is moderate at best — neither is a pharmaceutical-grade intervention. **Full comparison below.**

## Bottom Line

**If your primary issue is stress, poor sleep, or anxiety, and your testosterone is low as a result:** start with ashwagandha. The cortisol-testosterone connection is well-documented, and ashwagandha's cortisol-lowering effects are replicated across multiple trials.

**If your stress is well-managed but you have low libido, energy, or testosterone without an obvious cause:** tongkat ali may be more directly targeted. The evidence is thinner, but the mechanism is more direct.

**If you're not sure:** get your cortisol tested. If it's elevated, start with ashwagandha. If it's normal but testosterone is low, consider tongkat ali. In all cases, sleep, nutrition, and exercise matter more than either supplement.
