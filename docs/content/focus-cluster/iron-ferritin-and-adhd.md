# Iron/Ferritin and ADHD

Source article updated and expanded.

## Quick Facts

| Detail | Value |
|----------|----------|
| Nutrient | Iron |
| Primary Biomarker | Ferritin |
| Primary Mechanism | Dopamine synthesis via tyrosine hydroxylase |
| Strongest Evidence | Low ferritin and increased ADHD symptom severity |
| Typical Ferritin Target Discussed in RLS Literature | >50 ng/mL |
| Evidence Grade | B |
| Typical Time to Evaluate Response | 8–12 weeks |
| Major Safety Concern | Iron overload in non-deficient individuals |

## Study Registry

| Study | Type | Population | Outcome |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Konofal 2004 | Observational | Children with ADHD | Lower ferritin associated with greater symptom severity |
| Konofal 2008 | RCT | Low-ferritin ADHD children | Improvement in ADHD rating scales |
| Tseng 2018 | Meta-analysis | ADHD vs controls | Significantly lower ferritin in ADHD |
| Wang et al. | Meta-analysis | Pediatric ADHD | Iron deficiency associated with ADHD severity |
| Cortese et al. | Review | ADHD and sleep disorders | Strong ADHD-RLS-low ferritin overlap |
