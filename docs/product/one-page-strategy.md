# One-Page Product Strategy

Date: 2026-05-30

## Product direction

The Hippie Scientist should become the trusted decision layer between supplement curiosity and supplement purchase.

The product should not try to be a full supplement encyclopedia, personalized medical app, or broad product marketplace. Its core job is simpler:

> Help a skeptical supplement buyer choose a safe, evidence-aware shortlist for a specific goal and understand what product-quality signals to check before buying.

## Primary audience

Health-conscious supplement buyers who are willing to purchase but do not trust hype. They want to know what is plausible, what is risky, what form matters, and what to compare before they click out to a retailer.

## Core journeys

1. **Goal-led buyer:** “What should I try for sleep, stress, focus, mood, recovery, or gut comfort?”
2. **Ingredient-led researcher:** “Is ashwagandha, magnesium, L-theanine, or another ingredient worth considering for me?”
3. **Comparison-led decider:** “Which option is the better fit, and what should I look for if I buy?”

## MVP priorities

Build a goal-first revenue MVP with no route changes:

1. **Six homepage goals:** Sleep, Stress/Calm, Focus/Energy, Mood, Inflammation/Recovery, Gut Comfort.
2. **Priority goal decision tables:** best first options, situational options, avoid/caution options, evidence confidence, safety watchout, product-quality next step.
3. **Lightweight “Find my shortlist” flow:** 3 required questions plus safety context; no login, no diagnosis, no email wall.
4. **Top profile decision snapshots:** best if, avoid if, evidence level, form/quality signal, compare against.
5. **Product-quality CTA:** buying criteria and carefully limited affiliate paths only after evidence and safety context.

## What to avoid now

- Full marketplace or large vetted product database.
- Account system, saved plans, email-first retention, or shareable personal result URLs.
- Complex scoring engines, diversity rules, and editorial tooling before demand is proven.
- Multiple competing entry tools such as quiz, builder, planner, safety checker, and pathway explorer as equal primary CTAs.
- Affiliate-first modules before safety and evidence context.
- Large route consolidation as a prerequisite to revenue learning.

## First build sequence

1. Sleep decision table.
2. Stress/Calm decision table.
3. Focus/Energy decision table.
4. Decision snapshots for the top commercial herbs and compounds serving those goals.
5. Product-quality CTA and limited affiliate guidance for eligible ingredients.
6. Lightweight shortlist flow using the static decision tables.
7. Basic analytics for goal selection, shortlist completion, comparison/profile clicks, product-quality clicks, and affiliate outbounds.

## Product principle

Safety before monetization. Fewer choices before more data. Manual editorial shortlists before automated personalization. Static-first architecture always.
