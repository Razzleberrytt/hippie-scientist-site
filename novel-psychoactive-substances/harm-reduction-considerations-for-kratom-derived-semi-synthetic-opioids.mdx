# Harm Reduction Considerations for Kratom-Derived Semi-Synthetic Opioids

<NPSDisclaimer />

**Evidence Snapshot**
Several compounds derived from or related to kratom alkaloids, including concentrated 7-hydroxymitragynine and semi-synthetic substances such as MGM-15 and mitragynine pseudoindoxyl, have appeared in unregulated markets. Human safety data for these compounds is extremely limited.

**Strong Disclaimer**
This article is for informational and educational purposes only. These compounds have little to no human clinical safety data. This is not medical advice. They can carry serious and poorly characterized risks.

## Introduction

This article outlines general harm reduction considerations for individuals who may encounter these substances.

## Key Areas of Uncertainty

- Limited or absent controlled human safety studies
- Lack of established dosing information
- Unknown long-term health effects
- Limited understanding of dependence and withdrawal potential
- High variability in product composition

## General Harm Reduction Considerations

- **Potency differences**: Many of these compounds demonstrate higher mu-opioid receptor activity than mitragynine.
- **Product variability**: Unregulated products can vary significantly in composition and concentration.
- **Interactions**: Combining these substances with other central nervous system depressants increases risk substantially.
- **Dependence and withdrawal**: Opioid-acting substances carry a risk of physical dependence.

## Additional Considerations

- Testing resources, where available, may provide some information about product contents.
- Seeking medical help promptly in the event of adverse effects is important.
- Support resources for dependence and withdrawal exist.

## Conclusions

Kratom-derived semi-synthetic opioids represent a category of substances for which human safety data is extremely limited. Individuals who choose to engage with these substances should do so with awareness of these limitations.
