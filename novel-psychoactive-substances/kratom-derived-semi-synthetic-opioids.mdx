# Kratom-Derived Semi-Synthetic Opioids: What's Actually Happening

<NPSDisclaimer />

**Evidence Snapshot**
In addition to traditional kratom leaf products, more potent concentrated and chemically modified compounds have appeared in unregulated markets. These include forms of 7-hydroxymitragynine and semi-synthetic derivatives such as MGM-15 and mitragynine pseudoindoxyl. Human safety data for most of these substances is extremely limited.

**Strong Disclaimer**
This article is for informational and educational purposes only. These compounds have little to no human clinical safety data. This is not medical advice. They can carry serious and poorly characterized risks.

## Introduction

Traditional kratom products are made from the leaves of *Mitragyna speciosa*. In recent years, a growing number of products have appeared that contain concentrated or chemically modified versions of kratom alkaloids. These are often still marketed under the broad label of "kratom."

## Key Compounds

- **7-Hydroxymitragynine**: A potent metabolite of mitragynine. Concentrated forms show significantly higher opioid receptor activity.
- **MGM-15 (Dihydro-7-hydroxy mitragynine)**: A semi-synthetic compound with higher receptor activity than 7-hydroxymitragynine in laboratory studies.
- **Mitragynine Pseudoindoxyl**: A rearrangement product of 7-hydroxymitragynine that can also form in human plasma. It shows the highest mu-opioid receptor affinity among the three in available research.

## Why This Shift Matters

Higher potency increases the potential for opioid-like effects and associated risks. Product labeling can be unclear or misleading, and consumers may not realize they are using substances significantly different from traditional kratom leaf.

## Evidence Limitations

Human clinical data for these compounds remains very limited. Most available information comes from laboratory research, forensic detection, and post-market reports.

## Harm Reduction Considerations

- These compounds generally show higher opioid receptor activity than mitragynine.
- Product composition and dosing can be inconsistent or mislabeled.
- Combining these substances with other central nervous system depressants increases risk substantially.
- There is limited reliable information regarding dependence and interactions.

## Conclusions

The kratom market has expanded beyond traditional leaf products to include more potent concentrated and semi-synthetic compounds. Individuals who encounter these products should approach them with significant caution.
