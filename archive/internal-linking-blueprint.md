# Internal Linking Blueprint: The Hippie Scientist

## Strategic Linking Architecture

This document defines the internal linking strategy for scaling The Hippie Scientist from ~150 pages to 1,358 pages while maximizing topical authority, user navigation, and SEO performance.

---

## Linking Principles

### 1. Topical Clustering
- Links flow from broad (discovery) → narrow (depth)
- Hub pages link to 15-25 related subtopics
- Subtopic pages link back to hub + 3-5 related subtopics
- Goal: Create topical silos where content clusters reinforce each other

### 2. Authority Flow
- Hub pages accumulate external backlinks (SEO campaigns)
- Hubs distribute authority to condition pages via internal links
- Condition pages distribute authority to symptom bridges
- Symptom bridges feed back to conditions (circular reinforcement)

### 3. User Journey
- Discovery layer (guides, best-supplements-for) captures high-volume intent
- Links to condition hubs for deeper education
- Condition hubs link to symptom bridges (for alternative entry points)
- Symptom bridges link to specific herbs/compounds (research-phase users)
- All paths lead to comparison pages or stack builder (conversion)

### 4. Anchor Text Strategy
- Use descriptive, keyword-rich anchor text (never "click here")
- Primary keyword in anchor text (e.g., "best supplements for anxiety" not "anxiety solutions")
- Mix: exact match (20%), partial match (40%), branded/navigational (40%)
- Natural language: anchor text should read naturally in context

## Measurement & Optimization

### Key Metrics

**Per-page metrics:**
- Internal link click-through rate (CTR): Target 15-30%
- Links per page: Target 12-25 depending on type
- Inbound internal links: Target 5-15 per page
- Reciprocal link percentage: Target 80%+ (hub ↔ condition)

**Cluster-level metrics:**
- Hub page authority distribution: Can measure via Internal Link Juicer or SEMrush
- Condition page average ranking position: Track in Google Search Console
- Comparison page featured snippet capture: Target 30% minimum
- Question-to-condition click flow: Measure via GA4 event tracking

**System-level metrics:**
- Total internal links created: 1,000-1,200 (vs. 50-70 currently)

## FAQ: Linking Strategy

**Q: Will internal linking help SEO?**
A: Yes. Google uses internal links for crawl efficiency and authority distribution. Strategic linking:
- Helps crawlers discover new pages faster
- Distributes PageRank from high-authority hubs to condition pages
- Establishes topical relevance (co-citation of related topics)
- Improves user experience (navigation between related content)

**Q: How many internal links is too many?**
A: 25-30 is the practical limit for maintaining natural readability. Avoid:
- Footer links if they're not genuinely useful
- Links that disrupt sentence flow
- Excessive linking to low-relevance pages
- Creating noindex links for internal pages

**Q: Should I update existing pages with new links?**
A: Yes, prioritize:
- Month 1-2: Update herb/compound pages with links to new condition pages
- Month 3-4: Update guides with links to new content
- Month 4-6: Update condition pages with links to new comparisons, questions, buyer intent
- Ongoing: Update hub pages with links to new related content

**Q: How do I handle reciprocal links?**
A: Reciprocal links (A → B → A) are natural when:
- Hub ↔ Condition (always reciprocal)
- Comparison ↔ Individual items (always reciprocal)
- Herb ↔ Condition (always reciprocal)

Reciprocal links are healthy; they create topical clusters that Google loves.

**Q: What about cross-cluster links?**
A: Keep them to 1-2 per page maximum. Example:
- "Sleep problems caused by anxiety" links to anxiety page
- But most sleep page links stay within sleep cluster
- This maintains silo strength while allowing natural bridges
