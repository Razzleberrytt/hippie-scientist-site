SUPPLEMENT SAFETY CHECKLIST

Before you buy ANY supplement, verify:

BASIC SAFETY CHECKS

□ Not pregnant or breastfeeding
  (Unless supplement is specifically approved for pregnancy)

□ No ongoing medications
  (Supplements can interact with medications)

□ No upcoming surgeries
  (Many supplements affect bleeding/anesthesia)

□ No kidney, liver, or heart conditions
  (Some supplements stress these organs)

□ No known allergies to supplement ingredients
  (Even "natural" ingredients cause allergic reactions)


COMMON HIGH-RISK COMBINATIONS TO AVOID

❌ Magnesium + [Antibiotics, Bisphosphonates, Blood pressure meds]
   → Can reduce absorption of both substances

❌ Ashwagandha + [SSRIs, Anti-anxiety medications]
   → May increase sedation or interact with serotonin

❌ L-Theanine + [Stimulants, High-dose caffeine]
   → Can increase jitteriness/anxiety if combined wrong

❌ Blood-thinning supplements + [Warfarin, Aspirin]
   → Increases bleeding risk

❌ Any supplement + [Alcohol in large quantities]
   → Stresses liver; affects absorption


QUALITY SIGNALS TO CHECK BEFORE BUYING

Look for these on the supplement label or product page:

□ Third-party tested (NSF, USP, Informed Choice)
  → Independent testing for purity and potency

□ Clear "Supplement Facts" label
  → Shows exactly what's in each dose

□ GMP Certified (Good Manufacturing Practice)
  → Meets manufacturing standards

□ Expiration date listed
  → Shows freshness; older supplements degrade

□ Appropriate refund policy (30-day typical)
  → Shows company confidence in product

□ Standardized extract (if herbal)
  → Example: "Ashwagandha (15% withanolides)"
  → Ensures consistent potency


WHEN TO ASK A DOCTOR BEFORE STARTING

ALWAYS consult a healthcare provider if:

• You take prescription medications
• You have kidney, liver, or heart disease
• You're pregnant or breastfeeding
• You have upcoming surgery
• You have a bleeding disorder
• You have cancer or undergoing chemotherapy
• You're on immunosuppressants
• You have an autoimmune condition

It's not overkill—it's smart.
