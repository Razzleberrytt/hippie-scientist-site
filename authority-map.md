# Topical Authority Map: The Hippie Scientist

## Executive Summary

This document establishes a complete topical authority structure designed to scale the site from current content (~150 pages) to 2,000+ pages across four content clusters:

1. **Condition Hubs** (300 pages): Disease/disorder/health condition pages with symptom bridges
2. **Comparison Pages** (300 pages): Head-to-head botanical, compound, and supplement comparisons
3. **Question-Based Content** (300 pages): Answer engine optimization targeting "how/why/what" queries
4. **Buyer Intent Pages** (200 pages): Commercial keywords targeting supplement seekers at decision stage

## Part 5: Keyword Research Methodology

### Condition Page Keywords (300 pages)

**Format:** [Condition] + [Intent modifier]
- Examples:
  - "fibromyalgia natural treatment"
  - "fibromyalgia supplement"
  - "fibromyalgia herbs"
  - "fibromyalgia support"
  - "what helps fibromyalgia"
  - "foods for fibromyalgia"

**Research Tool**: Ahrefs/SEMrush keyword research
- Filter by: Monthly searches 200+, KD < 35
- Target long-tail (30+ characters)
- Exclude generic health content

### Comparison Page Keywords (300 pages)

**Format:** [Item A] vs [Item B]
- Examples:
  - "ashwagandha vs rhodiola for stress"
  - "valerian vs melatonin for sleep"
  - "curcumin vs boswellia"

**Research Tool**: Google Autocomplete + "vs" searches
- Filter by: Monthly searches 200+, KD < 40
- Prioritize high-commercial-intent pairs

### Question Page Keywords (300 pages)

**Format:** Question format queries
- Examples:
  - "how long does ashwagandha take to work"
  - "how much ashwagandha should I take"
  - "does ashwagandha work for anxiety"
  - "why am I so tired all the time"

**Research Tool**: Answer-the-Public + Ahrefs
- Filter by: Monthly searches 100+, KD < 30
- Focus on long-tail, conversational queries

- Examples:
  - "best supplements for sleep"
  - "best herbs for anxiety"
