Kanna (Sceletium tortuosum)

Kanna is a South African succulent revered for its uplifting, anxiolytic profile derived from a family of mesembrine alkaloids that modulate serotonin reuptake.

Active Constituents

Primary alkaloids include mesembrine, mesembrenone, mesembrenol, and tortuosamine. These compounds act as serotonin reuptake inhibitors and PDE4 inhibitors, producing calm alertness without significant stimulation.

Traditional & Modern Use

Indigenous Khoisan communities fermented and chewed kanna for social cohesion, stress relief, and endurance during hunts. Modern explorers experiment with sublingual extracts, nasal snuff, and vaporized resin concentrates for fast onset.

Preparation & Dosage

  • Chewable fermented leaf: 50–150 mg dried material.
  • Tea or infusion: 200–400 mg steeped 15 minutes.
  • Extract powder (20x): 15–30 mg sublingual.
  • Vaporized resin: One rice-sized dab for a brief, bright lift.

Always titrate slowly and wait at least 20 minutes between additional doses to avoid serotonergic overload.

Safety Notes

Avoid combining kanna with SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, or other serotonergic agents. Side effects may include mild jaw tension or lethargy at higher doses. Hydrate well and pair with grounding breathwork to integrate the relaxed focus.
