ADHD Supplement Starter Checklist

This checklist is for educational purposes only and is not medical advice. Supplements should not replace prescribed ADHD treatment. Talk with a qualified healthcare professional before starting supplements, especially if you take medication, are pregnant, have a medical condition, or are choosing supplements for a child.

Section 1: What to test before supplementing

  • Sleep quality baseline
  • Focus/attention baseline
  • Energy baseline
  • Anxiety/stress baseline
  • Current medications and supplement list
  • Caffeine intake
  • Diet/protein consistency

Section 2: Choose one starting lane

  • If sleep is the biggest issue: magnesium glycinate
  • If calm focus is the biggest issue: L-theanine
  • If long-term baseline support is the goal: omega-3
  • If brain fog is the biggest issue: citicoline
  • If caffeine causes anxiety: caffeine-free focus stack

Section 3: 4-week starter protocol

  • Week 1: Pick one supplement only and start low.
  • Week 2: Track sleep, focus, mood, anxiety, and side effects.
  • Week 3: Continue only if tolerated. Do not stack aggressively.
  • Week 4: Decide whether to keep, stop, or adjust.

Section 4: What to avoid immediately

  • High-dose multi-supplement stacks
  • Proprietary blends with hidden doses
  • Stimulant-heavy products
  • Products claiming to treat, cure, or replace ADHD medication
  • Starting several new supplements at once
  • Giving supplements to children without clinician guidance
