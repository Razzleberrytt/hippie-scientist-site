About The Hippie Scientist

An independent research site bridging traditional plant medicine and modern pharmacology — built for curious people who want more than anecdote.

What This Site Is

The Hippie Scientist is an educational resource focused on psychoactive botanicals, herbal nootropics, and the neurochemistry of plant compounds. We cover traditional use, active constituents, pharmacological mechanisms, safety considerations, and practical preparation methods.

The site is independent and not affiliated with any podcast, product line, or commercial supplement brand of a similar name. No products are sold here, and editorial coverage is not influenced by advertising or sponsorship.

Editorial Approach

🔬 Evidence-first Claims are grounded in peer-reviewed research wherever possible, with citations to PubMed and primary literature.
🌿 Traditional context Ethnobotanical and historical use is presented alongside modern data — neither dismissed nor uncritically accepted.
⚖️ Harm reduction Safety, contraindications, and dosing guidance are included for every herb covered. We do not encourage reckless use.
🔄 Ongoing updates Herb and compound data are versioned and updated as new research emerges. Entries carry a last-updated timestamp.

Data & Methodology

The herb and compound database currently contains 690 herbs and 391 compounds, sourced from published pharmacological literature, ethnobotanical databases, and primary research papers. Data completeness varies by entry — fields with missing citations are flagged.

For a full description of how data is collected, normalized, and quality-checked, see the Methodology page.

⚠️ Medical Disclaimer
Content on this site is for educational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider before using any herb or supplement, especially if you take medications or have underlying health conditions. See the full disclaimer.
