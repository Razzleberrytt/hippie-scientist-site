## Proposed claims

### scite-glycine-daytime-function-2026-07-11-003

- Predicate: `supports_outcome`
- Evidence: `human_obs`; confidence 0.77
- Sources: 1

Under short-term partial sleep restriction, 3 g of glycine before bedtime improved some next-day fatigue and performance measures versus placebo, but the study was small and does not show that glycine reverses the broader effects of chronic sleep loss.

### scite-glycine-evidence-limit-2026-07-12-004

- Predicate: `supports_outcome`
- Evidence: `human_obs`; confidence 0.91
- Sources: 3

Evidence for glycine as a sleep aid remains preliminary: trials are few, generally small, short, and concentrated in adults with subjective sleep dissatisfaction rather than patients with formally diagnosed chronic insomnia.

### scite-glycine-objective-sleep-2026-07-11-002

- Predicate: `supports_outcome`
- Evidence: `human_obs`; confidence 0.76
- Sources: 1

One small polysomnography crossover study reported shorter sleep-onset latency and selected sleep-quality improvements after 3 g of bedtime glycine, while overall sleep-stage proportions were not materially altered.

### scite-glycine-safety-2026-07-12-006

- Predicate: `has_safety_warning`
- Evidence: `human_obs`; confidence 0.78
- Sources: 2

Short sleep studies using 3 g near bedtime did not identify a strong next-day sedative signal, but they were too small and brief to establish long-term safety; gastrointestinal discomfort can occur with amino-acid supplements, and people who are pregnant, have significant kidney or liver disease, or take prescription medicines should seek clinical guidance before regular high-dose use.

### scite-glycine-subjective-sleep-2026-07-11-001

- Predicate: `supports_outcome`
- Evidence: `human_obs`; confidence 0.78
- Sources: 2

Small randomized crossover studies in adults dissatisfied with their sleep found that 3 g of glycine taken shortly before bedtime improved some subjective ratings of sleep quality and next-morning refreshment compared with placebo.

### scite-glycine-thermoregulation-2026-07-12-005

- Predicate: `supports_outcome`
- Evidence: `human_obs`; confidence 0.72
- Sources: 1

A proposed sleep-related mechanism is enhanced heat dissipation and the normal pre-sleep decline in core body temperature, but this mechanism is supported more strongly by physiological and preclinical work than by definitive human mediation studies.
